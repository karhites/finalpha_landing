EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•

The Solid State Transformer (SST) market is emerging as a critical technology for AI
data centers, with significant developments announced in late 2025. The supply chain
exhibits oligopolistic characteristics dominated by major semiconductor manufacturers
and power electronics companies. Interconnection delays (4-8 years) are the biggest
bottleneck, with behind-the-meter (BTM) solutions and flexible interconnection
strategies offering accelerated deployment timelines (12-18 months).


I. SOLID STATE TRANSFORMER TECHNOLOGY OVERVIEW
â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•

Market Size & Growth:
- Market projected to reach $585.78M by 2033 (Astute Analytica)
- Currently in experimental/conceptual stage for AI data centers
- NVIDIA's October 2025 White Paper describes SST as "ultimate technological form"
  for 800V DC AI data center power architecture

Key Technical Advantages:
- Direct medium-voltage (13.8-34.5 kV) to 800-1500V DC conversion
- >99% efficiency (vs. traditional transformers)
- Dramatic reduction in size, weight, and CO2 footprint
- Enables modular 2-5 MW building blocks
- Critical for gigawatt-scale AI datacenters

Technology Shift Drivers:
- AI exponential growth outstripping 54V data center power infrastructure
- Transformation toward high-density 800V powered data centers
- Need for rapid deployment and reduced interconnection times

A. THE CORE PROBLEM

Current State:
- Standard grid interconnection: 4-8 years timeline
- Nearly 2 TW of clean energy stuck in interconnection queues
- Queue represents 1.6x current total US grid capacity
- Biggest bottleneck for AI data center deployment

Cost Impact:
- Lengthy delays increase project costs
- Missed revenue opportunities during delay period
- Uncertainty in project timeline planning


B. SOLUTION #1: BEHIND-THE-METER (BTM) POWER GENERATION â­ PRIMARY SOLUTION

What It Is:
- Dedicated power generation directly connected to data center
- Bypasses traditional utility distribution systems
- Can include: solar, batteries, natural gas, fuel cells, nuclear

Key Benefits:
âœ“ Avoids 4-8 year grid interconnection delays
âœ“ Immediate deployment capability
âœ“ Predictable power supply without grid dependency
âœ“ Can be 100% renewable (solar + storage)

Market Adoption:
- McKinsey: 25-33% of incremental data center demand through 2030 will use BTM
- xAI, Meta, OpenAI: Already using BTM gas generation
- Equinix: Committed to onsite fuel cells

Technology Combinations:
1. Solar + Battery Storage
   - Co-located batteries provide grid support during peaks
   - Delivers renewable energy when grid needs it most
   - Reduces interconnection stress

2. Natural Gas Generation
   - Immediate deployment capability
   - High reliability for 24/7 operations
   - Bridge solution while renewables scale

3. Nuclear (Emerging)
   - Oklo, Nano Nuclear Energy developing portable micro reactors
   - Designed specifically for AI data center applications
   - Carbon-free baseload power

Key Companies in BTM:
- FlexGen (private): Battery storage + software orchestration
- Schneider Electric: BESS solutions for data centers
- Multiple nuclear startups: Oklo (OKLO), Nano Nuclear (NNE)


C. SOLUTION #2: FLEXIBLE INTERCONNECTION WITH BATTERIES â­ GAME CHANGER

What It Is:
- Take portion of load as "firm" utility-guaranteed power
- Remainder as "non-firm" interruptible capacity
- Batteries + software enable seamless switching during grid stress

Revolutionary Timeline:
- Traditional: 5-8 years
- Flexible interconnection: 12-18 months (6-18 months faster!)

How It Works:
1. Data center accepts some interruptible power allocation
2. FlexGen's HybridOS software optimizes battery storage
3. During grid stress/peaks, switch to battery power
4. Reduces peak draw on grid â†’ less infrastructure upgrade needed
5. Faster interconnection approval due to lower grid impact

Technology Requirements:
- Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS)
- Advanced software control (e.g., FlexGen HybridOS)
- Load flexibility capability in data center design
